When we publish content on the web, it's rarely a one-and-done process. Ideas develop, facts change, and our understanding deepens. Having a clear record of when updates occurred helps readers understand the context and currency of the information they're consuming.

## Testing Update Fields

This post has three recorded updates in its history. The `lastUpdated` field should reflect the most recent update date (March 1, 2025), while the `updates` array tracks all the historical update timestamps. This allows the system to:

- Show readers when content was last revised
- Track the evolution of a piece over time
- Potentially show a changelog or diff view in the future

## Why This Matters

Transparency about content updates builds trust with readers. When someone references information from an article, knowing when it was last updated helps them assess its relevance and accuracy. This is especially important for fast-moving fields like technology, science, and current events.

Keeping track of updates also helps content creators maintain accountability and provides a personal log of how their thinking has evolved on a particular topic.
